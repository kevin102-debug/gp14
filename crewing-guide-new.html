The Six Ps to Going Faster Before You Even Get on the Water

"Proper Preparation Prevents Pretty Poor Performance"

Championships have been lost owing to simple preparation mistakes. The most frustrating thing is the stupidity of it â€“ there's simply no excuse for missing the start, sailing the wrong course, or having sheets rigged incorrectly. Remember the Six Ps and be properly prepared for the race in hand.

Pre-Race Checklist

  • Arrive early - On water with time for practice beat before start
  • Rig correctly - Kite halyard & sheets outside everything, jib halyard shackle taped
  • Bungs in? - Simple but critical check
  • Sign on - Agree who's responsible for registration
  • Know the course - Marks, direction, laps, start/finish locations
  • Weather & tide - Forecast and tidal currents

On-Water Preparation

  • Check start line - Transits vs inner/outer marks (gain advantage)
  • Determine favored end - Which end is further upwind
  • Practice beat - Confirm wind direction and line bias
  • Check tide on marks - Sea sailing preparation
  • Practice kite hoist/gybe/drop - Confirm correct rigging
  • Get gun timing - 2 guns before start to sync watch

Pro Tip: Watch the Fleet Ahead

If another fleet starts before you, watch their start and see who performs best up the first beat â€“ valuable information for when you're on your way up there.

Personal Preparation

Clothing, nutrition, physical and mental readiness

Clothing

  • What works for you - Personal preference based on experience
  • Watch & snack - Mars bar before each race start
  • Layer strategy - Can always take off, but can't add what you don't have
  • Sun protection - Hat and cream (sunstroke spoils post-race beers!)
  • Hiking shorts - Your choice based on comfort

Physical & Mental

  • Hydration - Dehydration = loss of performance
  • Diet - Proper nutrition before racing
  • Sleep - Adequate rest the night before
  • Physical prep - Warm up and stretch
  • Mental focus - Visualization and concentration

Boat Preparation

Control lines, rigging, and system checks

Your job as crew can be made easier by having all control lines working properly. Communication & Coordination between Crew and Helm dramatically enhances boat performance. It is your responsibility to ensure everything is working properly.

Control Lines Checklist

  • 1
    Kicking Strap (Vang)
  • 2
    Downhaul (Cunningham)
  • 3
    Outhaul
  • 4
    Spinnaker Pole Uphaul
  • 5
    Spinnaker Pole Downhaul
  • 6
    Twining Lines (correctly set)
  • 7
    Jib Tension

Equipment Checks

  • Toe Straps - Correct length, not worn (falling out is embarrassing on video!)
  • Mast Setup - 18" top to goose neck black band, 21'10" to transom center
  • Jib Tension - 300lbs mean tension (starting point)
  • Tuning Guides - Speed Sails, Arrow Sail

Common Rigging Mistakes

The most common rigging error is kite halyard and sheets on wrong side of jibs.

  • â€¢ Solution: Kite goes outside everything
  • â€¢ Jib halyard shackle: Tape up to prevent kite halyard catching
  • â€¢ Kite halyard tension: Pull tight when setting rig tension (prevents looping in mast)

Racing Flags & Signals

Know the flags to avoid disqualification

Starting Sequence Flags

Time Signal Flag
5 min Warning Signal Class Flag
4 min Preparatory P, I, Z, or Black
1 min One Minute Prep flag removed
0 min Start Class flag removed

Preparatory Flags Explained

P Flag (Normal Start)

Standard preparatory signal

I Flag (Round the Ends)

If over in last minute, must sail around end mark

Z Flag (20% Penalty)

20% time penalty if over in last minute

Black Flag (DSQ)

DISQUALIFICATION if over in last minute!

Recall Flags

  • Individual Recall: First Substitute flag
  • General Recall: X flag

Other Important Flags

  • C Flag: Change of Course
  • AP (Red/White): Postponed
  • Blue Flag: Finish boat on station

Postponement 6-Minute Gun

If a start is postponed/abandoned and a flag is flying on committee boat, when that flag is removed it signals 1 minute to Warning Signal (6 minutes to start).
