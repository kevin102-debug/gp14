Pinnell & Bax Mast Setup Guide

Official rigging specifications for Selden Cumulus & Superspar M7 masts

Crew Weight: 21-24 Stone | Mast Manufacturer Specifications

Mast Types Covered

Selden Cumulus

Premium mast system with proven performance characteristics

Superspar M7

High-performance racing mast with optimized bend characteristics

Spreader Settings

Same settings for both Selden Cumulus and Superspar M7

Spreader Length (A)

375mm

Spreader Angle (B)

155mm

Measurement Note: Spreader length (A) is the total horizontal span. Spreader angle (B) is the perpendicular deflection from the mast centerline.

Mast Foot Position

Series 1

Mast heel as far aft in the step as possible

Maximum aft position

Series 11 (Mark 2)

Measure from aft point on transom centerline to aft bolt on mast step

2840mm

Precise measurement required

Series 11 Note: You may need to measure through the transom flaps to reach the aft point on the centerline.

Mast Rake & Rig Tension

Conditions Mast Rake Rig Tension
Light - 400 lbs
Medium 21'9½" - 10" 400 lbs
Heavy - 350 - 400 lbs

Measuring Mast Rake

1

Measure from top of mast to top of transom

2

Measure with rig tension on (400 lbs)

3

Find datum point: measure to top of gooseneck (18"), then swing tape to transom

4

After achieving rake, measure pre-bend: 5mm bend at spreader height

Rig Tension Strategy

Base setting: 400 lbs for most conditions

As wind increases: Add more rig tension until overpowered

When overpowered: Begin reducing tension back to 350-400 lbs range

Genoa Fairleads

Base Position

Fairleads from centreline - Middle of deck

Medium Winds

1 hole aft

From base position

Strong Winds (20+ knots)

2 holes aft

From base position

Purpose: Moving fairleads aft opens the upper leach of the sail, preventing the slot from choking in stronger winds.
